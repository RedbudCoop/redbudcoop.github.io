Mosey

Membership to reside at Redbud is open to all full-time Cornell students, including undergraduate, graduate, international, and transfer students. We welcome and strive to provide safe spaces for people of color, queer individuals, students with physical or mental disabilities, students with financial burdens, and students with other marginalized identities.

Selection

Admission to reside at Redbud for the following academic year is done via random selection. In order to enter our selection pool, you must submit a questionnaire and participate in Mosey. (Those who cannot participate in Mosey can arrange an interview with the current president.) Mosey consists of at least:

  • Attending Open House (or arrange a house tour with the president)
  • Attending two Mosey events (one of these can be the Open House)
  • Take part in a cook group for one house dinner

Housing Options

Our house consists of 19 singles, 6 doubles and 1 triple. Typically new house members will be placed in a double or triple with a roommate or two. Singles are usually reserved for returning house members.

Meal Plan

Our meal plan is run by the members of the meal plan. All house members are required to be a part of the meal plan, but the meal plan is open to members of the Cornell community. Redbuddies are required to cook on the meal plan unless otherwise stated in the constitution. External meal planners have the option of cooking or paying an additional fee to not cook on the meal plan.
